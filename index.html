üîß Daily Checks Are Critical

Pre-use inspections catch 70% of potential equipment failures. Never skip these checks, even when you're in a rush to meet production targets.

Essential Daily FLT Checks:

  • Tires and wheels - look for wear or damage
  • Hydraulics - check for leaks or unusual noises
  • Brakes - test effectiveness before moving loads
  • Warning devices - horn and flashing lights
  • Forks and attachments - check for cracks or bends
  • Seatbelt and ROPS - ensure proper function

Speed Limits Save Lives

üö® Warehouse Speed Limits

  • Production areas: Walking pace only (max 5mph)
  • Warehouse aisles: Maximum 8mph
  • Loading bays: 3mph maximum
  • Near pedestrians: Always reduce speed

Remember: Meeting OEE targets is never worth risking someone's safety!

‚öôÔ∏è Machine Safety & Lockout Procedures

‚ñº

üîí LOTO is Your Lifeline

Lock Out, Tag Out procedures prevent serious injuries from unexpected machine startup. Never bypass or ignore these procedures.

üìπ Video: LOTO Procedure Step-by-Step Demonstration (6:15 minutes)

New Equipment and Safety Updates

üîß Recent Changes to Our Operations

  • Stick pack machine installation: New interlocks and emergency stops
  • Line extensions: Updated guarding on blue/green lines
  • Automation upgrades: New light curtains and safety mats
  • Spare parts line build: Temporary safety procedures in place

Machine Guard Inspection Points:

  • Guards are secure and not damaged
  • Emergency stops are clearly marked and tested
  • Interlocks function properly - door opens = machine stops
  • Warning signs are visible and legible
  • Access to moving parts is completely prevented
  • Cleaning access points have proper procedures

üö® Never Override Safety Systems

Production pressure is real, but bypassing guards or interlocks is never acceptable. If safety systems are interfering with production:

  • Stop the line safely
  • Report to your Production Manager immediately
  • Contact Engineering for proper resolution
  • Document the issue for continuous improvement

üîê Click to review: 6-Step LOTO Procedure

  1. PREPARE: Identify energy sources and isolation points
  2. NOTIFY: Inform affected workers of shutdown
  3. SHUTDOWN: Turn off equipment using normal procedures
  4. ISOLATE: Switch off and lock energy sources
  5. TAG: Apply warning tags with your details
  6. VERIFY: Test that equipment cannot start

Remember: Only YOU can remove your own lock and tag!

  • Cleaning equipment: Wheels and handles

üö® Know Your Limits

Manual handling limits aren't just suggestions - they're based on research to prevent injury:

  • Men: Maximum 25kg in ideal conditions
  • Women: Maximum 16kg in ideal conditions
  • Repetitive lifting: Reduce limits by 30%
  • Awkward positions: Reduce limits by 50%
  • If in doubt: Get help or use mechanical aids

üèÉ Click to see: Preventing Repetitive Strain

Simple steps to prevent RSI:

  • Job rotation: Change tasks every 2 hours where possible
  • Micro-breaks: 30-second stretches every 30 minutes
  • Proper setup: Adjust work height to avoid bending
  • Tool selection: Use ergonomic tools with good grips
  • Report early: Don't ignore aches and pains

Remember: Early intervention prevents long-term injury!

ü™ú Working at Height - Ladders & Platforms

‚ñº

üìä UK Statistics Alert

Falls from height cause more workplace deaths than any other single cause in UK manufacturing. Even small heights can cause serious injury.

üìπ Video: Ladder Safety and the 4-to-1 Rule (3:20 minutes)

Ladder Inspection Before Every Use

Pre-Use Ladder Checks:

  • Rungs: No damage, securely attached, clean and dry
  • Stiles: No cracks, bends, or damage
  • Feet: Non-slip feet present and in good condition
  • Locks: Extension locks work properly
  • Labels: Safety information clearly visible
  • Weight rating: Suitable for user + tools/materials

üìê The 4-to-1 Rule

For every 4 units of height, the base should be 1 unit out from the wall:

  • Working at 4 metres high? Base should be 1 metre from wall
  • Working at 2 metres high? Base should be 50cm from wall
  • Always maintain 3 points of contact when climbing
  • Face the ladder when going up or down

üîç Alternative Options First

Before using a ladder, consider:

  • Mobile elevating work platforms (MEWPs) for extended work
  • Scaffold towers for repeated access
  • Long-handled tools to avoid height work
  • Ground-level solutions where possible
